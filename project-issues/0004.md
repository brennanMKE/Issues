## Description

The new tab bar from #0002 takes up far more space than it should. The two tab chips and the `+` button sit at the bottom of a roughly 600-point-tall empty region between the header and the stats bar — most of the window above the issue list is wasted black space. The chips themselves are correctly sized; the container around them is the problem.

## Steps to reproduce

1. Launch Issues.app with two folders open as tabs (e.g. `BucketKit` and `Issues`).
2. Observe the area below the header `Issues` title and above the `26 All · 9 Open · …` stats bar.

## Expected behavior

The tab bar should be a thin strip — roughly the height of the chips plus 6–8pt of vertical padding above and below (≈40–48pt total). The full content area (swimlanes / timeline / list) should occupy everything between the tab bar and the bottom of the window.

## Actual behavior

The tab bar's container appears to claim hundreds of points of vertical space. The chips are bottom-aligned within it, and the swimlane list is pushed offscreen — only a small slice of the issue cards is visible at the bottom of the window.

## Notes

- User's exact wording was "the horizontal space is way too big" — interpreting as "the area reserved for the tab bar is way too big" based on the screenshot, which clearly shows excess vertical space, not horizontal.
- Likely cause is a `Spacer()` or an unbounded `frame(maxHeight: .infinity)` in `TabBarView` (or in the wrapping container in `MainView`). The tab row should be intrinsic-height — wrap the `LazyHStack` of chips in a fixed-height row, no `maxHeight`.
- Check `Issues/Views/TabBarView.swift` and the slot in `Issues/Views/MainView.swift` where it's inserted between `HeaderView` and `StatsBarView`. The container's vertical resistance / hugging priority needs to favor a small intrinsic height.
- After the fix, verify the tab bar still looks correct with one tab and with many tabs (overflow into horizontal scroll should still work).
